Regulae Certaminis

Rules of the Debate

Summarized from the official rules PDF for the 2026 competition.

Open rules PDF Download PDF
I

Categories and Teams

Category I is for Classes XI and XII. Category II is for Classes IX and X. Each school team has two speakers: one for the motion and one against the motion.

II

Eligibility and Dress

Participants must be bona fide students on the school rolls and carry a school ID card or a letter from the Principal or Head of School. Formal wear is required; school uniforms are not to be worn.

III

Identity on Stage

Participating school names should not be displayed during the debate. Schools may be identified as Team A, Team B, Team C, and so on.

IV

Topic and Preparation

The topic is opened on the day of the competition and given one hour before the debate. No late participant may join after the topic is declared. Books and journals may be used; internet and electronic gadgets are not permitted.

V

Speaking and Rebuttal

Each speaker gets four minutes. Bells ring at three minutes and four minutes. After each speech, two minutes are allotted for rebuttal through short, precise questions from competing teams.

VI

Scoring Pattern

Each speaker is marked out of 50: Content 15, Argument/Rebuttal 10, Appropriateness of Language 10, Delivery 10, and Demeanor/Poise/Stance 5.

Gradus

Competition Stages

  1. Preliminary Stage: schools compete in pools; the winning team and first runner-up qualify for Selection.
  2. Selection Stage: qualified teams compete again; winners and first runners-up move to Semi Finals.
  3. Semi Finals: the winning team from each pool qualifies for the National Finals.
  4. National Finals: final national-level debate among the qualifying teams.

Tabulatio

Tabulation and Tie-Break

  • Judges award raw marks and rank every speaker on their own score sheet.
  • The tabulator records only the positions awarded by judges, not the raw marks.
  • The speaker and team with the lowest tabulation totals win their categories.
  • For a tie, remove each tied speaker's lowest rank and compare the remaining two ranks; the lower total wins.
  • If the tie remains, remove each tied speaker's lowest raw score and compare the remaining raw-score total; the higher total wins.
